What is Smart Grid Technology & How It Works?

A smart grid is an advanced electricity network that integrates digital communication, automation, and AI-driven data analysis to efficiently manage electricity flow. Unlike traditional power grids, smart grids adjust in real-time to balance demand and supply, reducing energy waste and improving reliability.

Key Features of Smart Grids:

  • Real-Time Energy Monitoring – Businesses track energy usage and detect inefficiencies.
  • Automated Demand Response – Reduces electricity costs by adjusting power consumption during peak hours.
  • Self-Healing Grid Networks – Detects and resolves power outages before they impact business operations.
  • Integration with Renewable Energy – Helps businesses use solar, wind, and battery storage more efficiently.
Smart grid adoption is expected to save UK businesses over £6 billion annually in energy costs by 2030.
Smart grids provide businesses with data-driven energy management, improving cost efficiency and power reliability.
