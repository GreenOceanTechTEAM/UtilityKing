Why Postcode Matters for Broadband

Broadband availability depends on: local infrastructure, Openreach coverage, Virgin Media network reach, alternative network (altnet) providers, and rural vs urban rollout. Two streets next to each other can have different options. That's why entering your postcode is the most accurate way to compare.

What Broadband Types Might Be Available?

1️⃣

Standard Fibre (FTTC)

Fibre to street cabinet · Copper to your home
Speeds up to 80 Mbps

Widely available across the UK. Typically the cheapest widely available option.

2️⃣

Full Fibre (FTTP)

Fibre directly to your property
100 Mbps up to 1Gbps

More reliable and future-proof. Availability expanding rapidly — especially in cities and new-build areas.

3️⃣

Cable Broadband

Virgin Media Network
Up to 1Gbps

Separate infrastructure. High-speed availability in many urban areas. Not available everywhere.

4️⃣

Alternative Networks

Hyperoptic, Community Fibre, Gigaclear, CityFibre
Competitive full fibre

Often offer competitive full fibre deals in select regions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q Why can my neighbour get faster broadband than me?

Infrastructure may differ slightly between properties, even on the same street.

Q Is full fibre available everywhere?

No. Coverage is expanding but still varies significantly by postcode.

Q Can I get broadband without a landline?

Yes. Many fibre and full fibre plans no longer require traditional landlines.

Q What if no fast broadband is available in my area?

You may consider mobile broadband or satellite as alternatives, though these differ in performance and cost.

Q How often should I check for new deals?

At least once per year — ideally when your contract ends.
