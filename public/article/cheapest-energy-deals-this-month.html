Why the Cheapest Energy Deals Change Every Month

There is no permanent cheapest energy supplier. Monthly changes happen because of market dynamics.

  • Wholesale price movements
  • Suppliers launching short-term fixed deals
  • Hedging strategy changes
  • Price cap updates (for variable tariffs)

A deal that was cheapest last month may already be gone. This is why live comparison matters more than headlines.

What "Cheapest" Actually Means in Energy

When suppliers advertise "cheap" energy, they often mean different things that may not suit your situation.

The True Cheapest Deal Is:

  • Lowest total annual cost
  • Based on your postcode

  • Includes standing charges properly
  • Considers tariff length

Monthly figures alone are misleading.
