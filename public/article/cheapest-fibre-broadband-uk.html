What Is Fibre Broadband?

In the UK, "fibre broadband" usually refers to one of two types:

1️⃣ Standard Fibre (FTTC)
Fibre to the cabinet · Copper cable to your home
Speeds up to 80 Mbps
2️⃣ Full Fibre (FTTP)
Fibre direct to your property
Speeds from 100 Mbps to 1Gbps · Faster and more reliable

Standard fibre is usually the cheapest option. Full fibre offers better performance — often for only slightly more.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q What is the cheapest fibre broadband in the UK right now?

Entry-level fibre deals usually start from £22–£25 per month, depending on postcode and promotions.

Q Can I get fibre for under £20?

Occasionally through limited offers or social tariffs, but standard pricing typically starts from £22+.

Q Is 67 Mbps enough?

For small households, yes. Larger families may benefit from 100 Mbps or more.

Q Are fibre prices fixed?

Many providers apply annual price increases linked to inflation. Always check terms.

Q Can I switch before my contract ends?

Yes, but early termination fees may apply.
