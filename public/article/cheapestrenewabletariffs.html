What Is Green Energy?

In the UK, green energy usually means:

Green Electricity

Generated from renewable sources such as wind, solar, and hydroelectric power. Backed by Renewable Energy Guarantees of Origin (REGOs).

Green Gas

Typically standard gas with carbon offsetting, or a partial biomethane blend. Electricity can be 100% renewable; gas cannot yet be fully renewable at scale.

Green Electricity Sources

UK green electricity comes from various renewable sources:

Wind Power

UK's largest renewable source, both onshore and offshore

Solar Energy

Growing rapidly with improved panel efficiency

Hydroelectric

Reliable renewable source from water flow

Biomass

Organic material converted to energy

Are Green Energy Tariffs Really 100% Renewable?

YES — for electricity.

UK green electricity tariffs are backed by REGOs (Renewable Energy Guarantees of Origin), which certify that renewable electricity has been generated and fed into the grid to match your usage.

REGO Certification System

This system is regulated and widely accepted, though suppliers differ in:

  • How transparently they source energy - Some suppliers own renewable generation assets
  • Whether they invest directly in renewables - Some fund new renewable projects
  • Supply chain transparency - Disclosure of energy sources
