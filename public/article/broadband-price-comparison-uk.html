Full Fibre vs Standard Fibre

Full fibre (FTTP) connects fibre directly to your home. Benefits: more stable speeds, less slowdown at peak times, lower latency, future-ready infrastructure. If the price difference is small (under £10/month), upgrading often makes sense. However, availability varies by postcode.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q Is higher Mbps always better?

Not always. Beyond your usage needs, extra speed may not improve everyday experience.

Q What is a good broadband speed in the UK?

For most households, 100–300 Mbps offers strong value.

Q Is 1Gbps worth it?

For heavy users or future-proofing, yes — but many homes won't fully utilise it.

Q Why do broadband prices increase mid-contract?

Many providers apply annual increases linked to inflation plus a fixed percentage.

Q Can I switch broadband easily?

Yes. Switching typically takes 1–2 weeks, and your new provider handles most of the process.
