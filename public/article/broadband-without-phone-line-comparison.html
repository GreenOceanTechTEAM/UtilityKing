What Is Broadband Without a Phone Line?

Broadband without a phone line (also called broadband-only) means:

  • No traditional copper landline
  • No line rental charges
  • Internet delivered via fibre or cable
  • Optional digital phone service (VoIP) if needed

Since the UK's digital switchover, many providers now deliver phone services through broadband rather than old copper networks. That means you can have internet only, or internet + digital voice, without paying for legacy line rental.

Who Should Choose Broadband Without a Phone Line?

✅

Broadband-only is ideal if:

  • You use your mobile for calls
  • You don't need a landline number
  • You want the simplest setup
  • You're a renter or student
  • You want lower monthly bills
📞

Phone line still makes sense if:

  • Elderly family members rely on it
  • You run a home business
  • You prefer a fixed number
  • Your alarm or medical equipment uses it

Frequently Asked Questions

Q Do I need a phone line for fibre broadband?

No. Full fibre and many cable services do not require traditional landlines.

Q Can I keep my number without a phone line?

Yes, if you choose a digital voice service that supports number transfer.

Q Is broadband-only faster?

Speed depends on infrastructure — not whether a phone line is included.

Q Are landlines being phased out?

Yes. Traditional copper phone lines are being replaced by digital voice systems.

Q Is broadband-only good for students?

Yes — especially when paired with shorter contracts or flexible plans.
