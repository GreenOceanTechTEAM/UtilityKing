How to compare cashback deals properly

The biggest mistake

Looking at cashback — not total cost.

Deal A: £30/month · £100 cashback

24-month: £720 – £100 = £620

Deal B: £27/month · No cashback

24-month: £648

Deal A cheaper overall

The calculation

Always calculate:

(Monthly price × contract length) – cashback

Example £34 × 24 = £816
Less cashback – £100
Total £716

Watch out for price rises

CPI + 3.9%

Many cashback deals still include annual increases. A £100 reward may be offset by a £3/month increase in year two — £36 extra over 12 months.

Key point

Cashback doesn't cancel future price rises.

Contract length & cashback

Longer commitment

Cashback is usually offered on:

  • 18-month contracts
  • 24-month contracts
Shorter contracts

Rarely include high cashback. Longer commitment = bigger incentive.

When cashback is worth it

✅ Strong value if:
  • Monthly price is competitive
  • Contract terms are clear
  • No excessive setup fees
  • Claim process is straightforward
🎯 Especially useful when:
  • Switching at contract end
  • Offsetting setup costs
  • Moving home
