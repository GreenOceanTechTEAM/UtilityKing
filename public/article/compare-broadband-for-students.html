What Makes Broadband Different for Students?

Students typically:

  • Rent for 9–12 months
  • Live in shared houses (3–6 people)
  • Stream heavily (Netflix, YouTube, Prime)
  • Game online
  • Work remotely or attend online lectures

That combination means: you need enough speed for multiple users — but not a long contract that extends past your tenancy.

Often cheaper
Simpler
No unused phone charges

How to Split Student Broadband Bills

To avoid arguments:

  • Nominate one account holder
  • Set up a shared payment system
  • Use bill-splitting apps
  • Agree contract length upfront

Clear communication prevents disputes later.

⚠️

Hidden Costs Students Should Watch For

Before signing up, check:

Setup or activation fees
Router delivery charges
Mid-contract price rises
Early termination fees
Equipment return policy

The cheapest monthly price isn't always the cheapest overall deal.

🚪

Moving Out Early: What Happens?

If you leave before the contract ends:

  • Early exit fees may apply
  • Remaining housemates may continue contract
  • Service may be transferable

If unsure, rolling contracts may be safer — though more expensive.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q Can students get 9-month broadband contracts?

Some providers offer flexible options, but most standard contracts are 12 months.

Q Is rolling broadband better for students?

For short-term stays, yes — but more expensive monthly.

Q Can we transfer broadband to new tenants?

Typically no — contracts are linked to account holder.

Q Is full fibre worth it for students?

If competitively priced and available, yes — especially for larger houses.

Q How long does installation take?

Usually 1–2 weeks.
