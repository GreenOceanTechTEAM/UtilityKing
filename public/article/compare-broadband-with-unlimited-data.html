DEFINITION

What does "unlimited" mean?

Typically includes

  • No monthly data cap
  • No extra charges
  • No automatic cut-off

May include (mobile)

  • Fair Usage Policies
  • Traffic prioritisation
  • Speed management during congestion
COMPARISON

Fibre unlimited vs mobile unlimited

Fibre Broadband (FTTP & FTTC)

  • Genuinely unlimited
  • No routine throttling
  • Stable peak performance
  • Best for heavy/family use

4G & 5G Unlimited

  • Unlimited in theory
  • May reduce priority at peak
  • Speed fluctuates
  • Best for flexible/temporary setups

FINE PRINT

Fair Usage Policies explained

Even unlimited plans may include terms like:

"Speeds may be reduced during busy periods."

This is traffic management — full fibre networks rarely need this, mobile networks use it frequently.

COMMON QUESTIONS

FAQs

Is unlimited broadband truly unlimited?

For fibre plans, generally yes.

Can unlimited broadband be throttled?

Rarely on fibre — more common on mobile.

Is unlimited necessary for light users?

Not always — capped plans can be cheaper.

Does unlimited cost more?

Often similar pricing to older capped plans.

Can unlimited still slow at peak?

Yes — due to congestion, not data limits.

Which unlimited plan is best?

Full fibre 300 Mbps for most families.
