What Is Fibre Broadband?

📡

1️⃣ Part-Fibre (FTTC)

Fibre to the Cabinet
  • Fibre runs to the street cabinet
  • Copper cable connects cabinet to your home
  • Speeds typically 35–80 Mbps
Most widely available fibre type
⚡

2️⃣ Full Fibre (FTTP)

Fibre to the Premises
  • Fibre runs directly into your property
  • Speeds from 100 Mbps up to 1Gbps
  • More reliable and lower latency
Expanding rapidly — check availability

Is Full Fibre Worth It?

If available in your postcode, full fibre often provides more consistent speeds, less peak-time slowdown, better upload speeds, and lower latency for gaming and video calls. If upgrading costs less than £10 extra per month, it's often worth considering. However, availability varies significantly across the UK.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q What is the difference between fibre and full fibre?

Standard fibre (FTTC) connects fibre to your street cabinet, then copper to your home. Full fibre (FTTP) connects fibre directly to your property.

Q Is fibre broadband available everywhere in the UK?

Most areas have standard fibre. Full fibre availability depends on infrastructure rollout.

Q What speed counts as superfast broadband?

Speeds above 30 Mbps are generally considered superfast.

Q Is 100 Mbps enough?

For most families, yes. It supports multiple devices and 4K streaming comfortably.

Q Can I switch before my contract ends?

Yes, but early exit fees may apply. Always check your current agreement.
