Introduction: Why Switching Energy Supplier Still Saves Money

Switching energy supplier is one of the simplest ways UK households can reduce their bills — yet millions of people still haven't switched in years. In 2026, the energy market is more competitive than it has been for some time, with fixed deals returning, green tariffs becoming cheaper, and suppliers offering aggressive pricing to win new customers.

Households that regularly switch energy suppliers typically save £200–£500 per year compared with those who stay on standard variable tariffs. Despite this, many people delay switching due to uncertainty, fear of disruption, or confusion around the process.

The Reality Is Simple

Switching energy supplier in the UK is safe, regulated, fast, and free in most cases.

This step-by-step guide explains exactly how to switch, how long it takes, what you need, and how to avoid common mistakes — so you can switch with confidence and maximise your savings.

Is It Safe to Switch Energy Supplier?

YES Energy switching in the UK is fully regulated by Ofgem, and the process has been simplified significantly in recent years.

Step-by-Step: How to Switch Energy Supplier in the UK

Follow these six simple steps to switch your energy supplier and start saving:

1

Gather Your Energy Information

Before comparing deals, have the following ready:

  • Your postcode
  • Current supplier name
  • Estimated annual electricity and gas usage (kWh)
  • Whether you want dual fuel or separate suppliers

You can find usage figures on a recent bill or annual statement. Using real usage data ensures accurate comparisons.

2

Compare Energy Deals

Use an energy comparison service that shows:

  • Total annual cost (not just unit rates)
  • Standing charges
  • Contract length
  • Exit fees
  • Tariff type (fixed or variable)

UtilityKing focuses on location-specific pricing and real annual costs, helping households see what they're actually likely to pay.

3

Choose the Right Tariff

When choosing a deal, consider:

Price certainty
Fixed vs variable
Contract length
12-24 months typically
Supplier reputation
Customer service ratings
Green energy options
Renewable choices

In 2026, competitive fixed tariffs are often the best option for most households.

4

Apply to Switch

Once you select a tariff:

  • Apply online (usually takes 5–10 minutes)
  • Provide basic personal details
  • Confirm your current supplier

Your new supplier will handle the rest. The entire application process is streamlined and straightforward.

5

Take a Final Meter Reading

You'll be asked to provide meter readings around the switch date. This ensures:

  • Your final bill with the old supplier is accurate
  • You only pay for what you've used

Smart meters usually send readings automatically, making this step even easier.

6

Switch Complete

Most switches complete within 2–5 working days. You'll receive:

  • A final bill from your old supplier
  • A welcome pack from your new supplier
  • Confirmation of your new direct debit setup

Your direct debit will automatically transfer to the new supplier.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does switching energy supplier take?

Typically 2–5 working days from application to completion. The actual switch date (when your new supplier takes over) will be confirmed during the application process.

Does switching cost anything?

No. Switching is free in most cases. There are no fees to switch between suppliers, though some fixed tariffs may have exit fees if you leave before the contract ends.

Will switching affect my credit score?

No. Energy switching does not impact your credit rating. Suppliers may perform a soft credit check, but this doesn't affect your score.

Can I switch if I owe money?

You may need to clear debt or agree a repayment plan, depending on the supplier and amount owed. Most suppliers require debts under £500 to be cleared before switching.
