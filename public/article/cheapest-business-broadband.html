What is the cheapest business broadband in the UK?

Entry‑level business fibre deals typically start from £28–£40 per month (excl. VAT) for speeds around 100 Mbps. Pricing depends on postcode, infrastructure (FTTC vs FTTP), contract length, setup fees, and whether a static IP is included.

Cheapest full fibre business broadband

If full fibre (FTTP) is available: higher upload speeds, lower latency, better reliability. In some areas, full fibre business plans are only slightly more expensive than standard fibre — making them better long‑term value.

Upload speed: where business plans shine

Upload is crucial for sending large client files, hosting servers, video conferencing, cloud backups, VoIP. Standard fibre upload: 10–20 Mbps Full fibre upload: 100–900 Mbps For cloud‑dependent businesses, full fibre is worth the extra.

Hidden costs to watch

  • VAT not always included in headline pricing
  • Setup or installation fees
  • Router / hardware charges
  • Static IP costs
  • Early termination fees
  • Mid‑contract price rises

When to consider a leased line

If your business depends on guaranteed uptime, hosts servers, handles critical data — a leased line offers dedicated connection, symmetrical speeds, SLA‑backed uptime. But pricing starts from £200+ per month. Not necessary for most small businesses.

Who can use the cheapest business broadband?

Best suited for sole traders, freelancers, home‑based businesses, small offices (1–5 staff), startups. Larger companies may need higher‑tier solutions.

Frequently asked questions

Is business broadband tax deductible? Yes — broadband costs are typically allowable business expenses (confirm with your accountant).
Is residential broadband cheaper? Yes — but without SLAs or business‑grade support.
Is full fibre worth it for business? In most cases, yes — especially for upload‑heavy operations.
How long does installation take? Typically 7–14 days.
Can I switch business broadband easily? Yes — but check notice periods and exit fees.
