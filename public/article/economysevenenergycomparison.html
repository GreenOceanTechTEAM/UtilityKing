What Is Economy 7 Energy?

Economy 7 is a type of electricity tariff that offers:

7 hours of cheaper electricity overnight

Typically between 11pm and 8am, exact hours vary by region.

Higher rates during the day

Standard or above-standard rates for 17 hours daily.

It was originally designed for homes that use most of their electricity at night, such as properties with storage heaters, immersion heaters, or electric-only heating systems.

In 2026, Economy 7 still exists — but it is not automatically cheaper for every household.

How Economy 7 Tariffs Work

An Economy 7 tariff splits electricity prices into two rates:

Night Rate (Off-Peak)
30–60% Cheaper

Applies for 7 hours overnight

Typically 11pm – 8am (varies by region)

Day Rate (Peak)
Higher Than Standard

Applies for the remaining 17 hours

Higher than standard tariff rates

👉 The key to saving money on Economy 7 is using at least 35–40% of your electricity at night.

Who Should Still Use Economy 7 in 2026?

Economy 7 Makes Sense For:

  • Flats with electric storage heaters
  • Properties without gas supply
  • Homes with immersion heaters
  • EV owners charging overnight
  • Households using timers to control usage

Economy 7 Usually Worse For:

  • Most usage during the day
  • Work from home with daytime electricity use
  • Homes with gas central heating
  • Low overnight usage patterns
