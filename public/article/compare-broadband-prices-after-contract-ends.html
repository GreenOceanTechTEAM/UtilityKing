What Happens When Your Broadband Contract Ends?

Most broadband contracts in the UK last:

  • 12 months
  • 18 months
  • 24 months

Once your minimum term ends:

  • Your service continues
  • But your promotional discount ends
  • You're moved onto a standard monthly rate

This rate is usually much higher than your original deal.

💰

How Much Do Prices Increase After Contract Ends?

Here's a typical example:

During Contract After Contract Ends
£28/month (150 Mbps) £42/month
£25/month (67 Mbps) £38/month
That's an increase of £10–£17 per month — £120–£204 per year. Many customers don't realise the increase until they review their bill.
🏦

Why Do Providers Increase Prices?

Promotional deals are designed to attract new customers. After the contract ends:

Discounts expire
Providers assume inertia
Standard pricing applies
This is often called a "loyalty penalty." New customers usually get better pricing than existing out-of-contract customers.
⚖️

Should You Renegotiate or Switch?

Option 1: Renegotiate
  • Call and ask for best renewal offer
  • Ask if they can match new customer pricing
  • Inquire about speed upgrades
Retention teams sometimes offer competitive deals — but not always as strong as switching offers.
Option 2: Switch Providers
  • Access to promotional pricing
  • Potential speed upgrade
  • Fresh contract incentives
Switching often saves more — especially if full fibre is now available.

What About Mid-Contract Price Rises?

Even during contracts, many providers apply annual increases (often CPI + 3.9%). After your contract ends, you may face both expired discounts and prior increases — making reviewing your deal even more important.
