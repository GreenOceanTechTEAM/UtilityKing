What Is a Broadband and Phone Deal?

A broadband and phone deal (often called a "bundle") combines home broadband connection and landline phone service.

The phone element may include:

  • Pay-as-you-go calls
  • Inclusive evening/weekend calls
  • Anytime call packages
  • International call add-ons

Since the UK's digital phone switchover, most landlines now operate via broadband (VoIP), not traditional copper phone lines.

📞

Do You Still Need a Landline?

Many households don't use their landline regularly. However, a phone bundle may still be useful if:

💼 You work from home
📞 You prefer a fixed number
👵 Elderly family members use it
🔄 Backup communication option
🔔 Alarm system connects via landline

If you never use a home phone, broadband-only deals may offer better value.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

When comparing broadband and phone deals in the UK, check for:

Setup or activation fees
Mid-contract price rises (CPI + 3.9%)
Call charges outside allowance
Contract length (often 24 months)
Early exit fees

Full Fibre and Digital Voice

The UK is moving towards full fibre and digital voice services. This means:

  • Phone services run through your broadband connection
  • Traditional copper landlines are being phased out
  • Calls may require a router connection

If upgrading to full fibre, your phone line may operate differently than before.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q Is it cheaper to bundle broadband and phone?

Sometimes yes. Some providers price bundles very competitively.

Q Can I keep my landline number?

Yes, in most cases you can transfer your existing number.

Q Are landlines being switched off?

Traditional copper networks are being phased out, but digital voice services replace them.

Q Do I need a phone line for broadband?

Many fibre and full fibre plans no longer require traditional landlines.

Q Can I switch before my contract ends?

Yes, but early termination fees may apply.
