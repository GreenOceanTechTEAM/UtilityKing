Fastest Broadband in the UK

The fastest widely available broadband in the UK is full fibre (FTTP), offering speeds up to 1Gbps (1000 Mbps). Unlike standard fibre (FTTC), full fibre connects directly to your property via fibre optic cable — delivering more stable speeds, lower latency, and better performance during peak times. Availability depends on your postcode. Urban areas have greater coverage, while rural areas may still rely on part-fibre infrastructure.

Hidden Fees to Watch Out For

Many comparison sites highlight the lowest monthly figure — but not the real cost. Here are common hidden fees:

Mid-contract price rises (CPI + 3.9%)
Setup or activation fees
Router delivery charges
Early exit penalties
Price increases after promotional period

At Utility King, we recommend reviewing total 18 or 24-month cost, contract terms, and cancellation policy. Transparency builds better long-term savings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q What is full fibre broadband?

Full fibre (FTTP) connects fibre optic cable directly to your property, delivering faster and more reliable speeds than part-fibre connections.

Q How long does broadband switching take?

Typically 1–2 weeks. Some providers offer faster installation depending on infrastructure.

Q Will I lose internet during switching?

Usually no. Most switches are seamless and scheduled.

Q What speed do I need for Netflix?

For 4K streaming, at least 25 Mbps per device is recommended.

Q Is fibre available everywhere?

Not yet. Coverage is expanding rapidly, but availability depends on your postcode.
