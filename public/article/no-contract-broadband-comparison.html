What Is No Contract Broadband?

No contract broadband means:

  • You're not tied into a long-term agreement
  • You can cancel with 30 days' notice
  • No early termination penalties
  • Typically higher monthly pricing

It's often marketed as 30-day broadband, rolling contract broadband, or flexible broadband. You still pay monthly — but you're free to leave.

Full Fibre on No Contract Deals

Some providers offer full fibre on rolling contracts — but expect to pay a premium. Benefits still include:

Higher upload speeds
Lower latency
Better reliability

If performance matters more than cost, this can be worthwhile.

🔧

Setup Fees & Equipment Costs

Rolling contracts sometimes include:

Higher upfront setup fees
Router purchase costs
Activation charges

Because providers aren't recovering costs over 24 months, they may charge more upfront. Always check installation fees, router charges, and delivery costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q Is no contract broadband more expensive?

Yes, typically £5–£10 more per month than long-term contracts.

Q Can I cancel anytime?

Usually with 30 days' notice.

Q Is full fibre available on rolling contracts?

Sometimes — availability depends on provider and postcode.

Q Are there hidden fees?

Some providers charge setup or router fees.

Q Is rolling broadband good for students?

Yes, especially if staying less than 12 months.

Who Should Choose No Contract Broadband?

Choose rolling broadband if:

  • You value flexibility
  • You're renting short-term
  • You expect to move soon
  • You want to avoid early exit fees

Choose 12–24 month contracts if:

  • You want lower monthly costs
  • You're settled long-term
  • You're budget-focused
