Types of EV Chargers & Choosing the Right Solution for Your Business

Before installing EV charging stations, businesses must understand the different charger types and select the most suitable option.

Slow Chargers (AC Charging – 3-7 kW)

  • Best For: Overnight charging for business fleets, employee parking, and residential buildings.
  • Charging Time: 6-12 hours for a full charge.
  • Installation Cost: Lower than fast or rapid chargers.

Fast Chargers (AC Charging – 7-22 kW)

  • Best For: Workplace charging and retail locations where vehicles are parked for a few hours.
  • Charging Time: 3-6 hours for a full charge.
  • Installation Cost: Moderate—ideal for most business applications.

Rapid Chargers (DC Charging – 50-350 kW)

  • Best For: Fleet depots, public charging stations, and highway service locations.
  • Charging Time: 80% charge in 20-40 minutes.
  • Installation Cost: Higher due to grid connection requirements, but ideal for businesses needing quick turnaround times.
Businesses that install a mix of fast and rapid chargers ensure better energy management and meet diverse customer needs.
Choosing the right chargers depends on business location, energy demand, and fleet usage patterns.
