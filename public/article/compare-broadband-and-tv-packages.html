Fibre vs full fibre in TV bundles
Standard (FTTC)

⬇️ up to 67–80 Mbps, suitable for HD streaming. Often in entry‑level bundles.

Full fibre (FTTP)

⬇️ 100–1000 Mbps, better for 4K streaming, stable under heavy use – increasingly available.

Is it cheaper to bundle?

Example comparison (monthly):
Bundle: 150 Mbps + TV = £50
Separate: broadband £28 + streaming services £15–20 = £43–48
➡️ Bundling may cost more depending on channels required.

Broadband speed needed for TV streaming

📺 HD streaming: ~5 Mbps per stream.
4K streaming: ~25 Mbps per stream.
Multiple 4K streams: 100–150 Mbps minimum; 300 Mbps for larger households.

Contract length & equipment
Most bundles: 18–24 months. Longer contracts reduce monthly cost but reduce flexibility. Check mid‑contract price rises (CPI +3.9% common), early termination fees, equipment return policies.
Sports & premium add‑on costs

⚽ Sports packages often add £20–35/month. 🎥 Cinema packages add £10–20/month. These significantly increase total contract cost – always calculate full monthly commitment.
