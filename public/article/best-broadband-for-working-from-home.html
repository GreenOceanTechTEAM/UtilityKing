What Broadband Speed Do You Need to Work From Home?

The right speed depends on how you work.

Basic Remote Work
Emails, browsing, admin – 35–67 Mbps sufficient
Frequent Video Calls
Zoom, Teams, Meet – 50–100 Mbps recommended
Large File Uploads
Cloud-based roles – 100–300 Mbps ideal
Multiple Remote Workers
150–300 Mbps minimum, 300 Mbps+ for smooth calls

If your household combines remote work with streaming and gaming, speed requirements increase further.

☁️

Why Upload Speed Is Critical for Remote Work

Most broadband adverts highlight download speed. But upload speed matters just as much for video calls, sending large attachments, cloud backups, VPN connections, and file sharing.

Standard fibre (FTTC)

10–20 Mbps

Full fibre (FTTP)

100–900 Mbps
If you regularly upload large files or host meetings, full fibre offers a significant advantage.
🔌

Fibre vs Full Fibre for Working From Home

Standard Fibre (FTTC)
Up to 80 Mbps
  • Moderate upload speeds
  • Suitable for light to moderate remote work
  • May fluctuate during peak hours
Full Fibre (FTTP)
100–1000 Mbps
  • Much higher upload speeds
  • Lower latency
  • More stable during busy times
  • Future-proof infrastructure

Reliability Matters More Than Raw Speed

For remote work, consistency is more important than peak speed. Look for:

Full fibre infrastructure
Low latency
Strong upload speeds
Stable peak-time performance

If your connection slows every evening, infrastructure — not speed — may be the issue.

Other Factors to Consider

Router Quality

A strong router improves internal Wi-Fi coverage.

Wired vs Wi-Fi

For important meetings, wired Ethernet connections are more stable.

Contract Flexibility

If renting, consider 12-month contracts or flexible options.

Mid-Contract Price Rises

Many providers increase prices annually (CPI + 3.9%).
