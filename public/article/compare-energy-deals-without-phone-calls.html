First Principles: What Is Needed to Compare Energy Deals?

Energy comparison is mathematical.

Every tariff is built from two components:

Unit rate (price per kWh)

Standing charge (daily fixed cost)

Your total annual energy cost is calculated as:

(Annual usage × Unit rate) + (Standing charge × 365)

To perform this calculation accurately, you only need:

Your postcode

Electricity usage (kWh)

Gas usage (kWh)

Current tariff (optional)

None of this requires a phone conversation. Energy pricing is formula-based — not negotiation-based.

Is Switching Energy Supplier Online Safe?

Yes.

Switching energy supplier:

Does not interrupt electricity or gas supply

Does not require engineering work

Is regulated by national energy authorities

Includes a cooling-off period

Your physical energy connection does not change. Only your billing provider changes. Switching online is no less secure than switching by phone — and often more transparent because everything is documented digitally.

When Might a Phone Call Be Necessary?

In rare cases, phone contact may be required if:

You have outstanding energy debt

Your meter type is unusual

Your property is newly registered

There are data mismatches in national databases

For standard domestic switches, however, the entire process can remain digital.
