Top 5 Costly Mistakes to Avoid

1

Staying on Variable

The default tariff is rarely optimal. It's designed for convenience, not savings.

2

Wrong Tariff Type

Choosing "cheap standing charge" deals that have high unit rates—disastrous for high usage.

3

Ignoring Unit Rates

4

Dual Fuel Assumption

Annual Review Neglect

⚠️ Special Considerations for Large Households

Electric-Heavy Homes (EVs, Heat Pumps): Prioritize competitive electricity unit rates. Smart tariffs only benefit if you can shift significant usage to off-peak hours.

Economy 7: Usually poor value for families—high daytime rates outweigh cheap nighttime rates.

Renters: You have the legal right to switch supplier if you pay the bill. Opt for no-exit-fee tariffs for maximum flexibility.
