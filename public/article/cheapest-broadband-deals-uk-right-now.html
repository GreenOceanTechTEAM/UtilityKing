The key is reviewing the total contract cost, not just the monthly price.

Why Prices Increase Mid-Contract

Many providers apply annual price rises. Typically: CPI inflation + 3.9%.

On a £28/month plan, that could increase to over £31 within a year.

When comparing the cheapest broadband deals, check: Is pricing fixed? Are increases guaranteed? Is there a price freeze? Understanding this protects long-term savings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q What is the cheapest broadband in the UK right now?

Entry-level fibre deals start from around £22/month, depending on postcode and promotional offers.

Q Can I get broadband for under £20 per month?

Occasionally through limited-time offers or social tariffs, but standard market pricing typically starts from £22–£25.

Q What are social tariff broadband deals?

Social tariffs are discounted broadband plans for households receiving certain government benefits. These can offer lower monthly pricing and flexible contracts.

Q Is full fibre cheaper now?

Yes. Full fibre pricing has become far more competitive, often only slightly more expensive than standard fibre.

Q Do comparison sites cost extra?
