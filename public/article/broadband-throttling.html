THE BASICS

What is broadband throttling?

🔧 Throttling means your provider intentionally reduces your speed, usually due to heavy usage, network congestion, or Fair Usage Policy limits.
Heavy data usage Network congestion FUP limits Traffic type
KEY DIFFERENCE

Throttling vs congestion

Throttling

  • Provider deliberately limits speed
  • Written in terms & conditions
  • May apply after usage threshold

Congestion

  • Network overloaded at peak
  • Speeds drop temporarily
  • Affects many users in area

Net neutrality limits discriminatory throttling. Most fibre providers operate without routine throttling.
