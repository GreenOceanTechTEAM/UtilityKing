Understanding the process and its benefits

    What You'll See in Instant Comparison Results

    A breakdown of the key information displayed

    A proper online energy comparison should clearly show:

    1. Estimated Annual Cost : Based on your usage and tariff rates. This is the most important figure for comparison.
    2. Unit Rates (p/kWh) : The single biggest factor in your bill, especially for medium and high usage homes.
    3. Standing Charges : Daily fixed costs that vary by region. More important for low usage homes.
    4. Tariff Type : Fixed, variable, green/renewable, or Economy 7/smart tariffs with clear labeling.
    5. Contract Details : Contract length, exit fees, and terms to avoid costly mistakes.

    Fixed vs Variable Results: What Instant Comparisons Reveal

    Understanding the key differences at a glance

    Fixed Tariffs

    • Price locked for contract duration
    • Frequently undercut the Ofgem price cap
    • Protection against market volatility
    • May have exit fees for early switching

    Variable Tariffs

    • Prices can change with the market
    • Usually more expensive for most households
    • No exit fees for switching
    • Track the Ofgem price cap

    Instant comparisons make it immediately clear whether switching is worthwhile by showing both options side-by-side with their true annual costs.

    Comparing Energy Deals for Different Households

    Tailored approaches for different needs

    Low Usage Homes

    Standing charges matter more than unit rates. Look for tariffs with low daily fixed costs.

    High Usage Homes

    Unit rates dominate savings. Focus on the lowest p/kWh charges for maximum benefit.

    Renters

    Flexibility and no exit fees often matter most. Month-to-month tariffs may be preferable.

    EV Owners

    Special EV tariffs or overnight rates can provide significant savings for charging.
