What Type of Meter Do You Have?

Identifying your meter for accurate comparison

Before comparing energy prices, it helps to know your meter type. The most common non-smart options are:

1. Standard Credit Meter

  • Manual readings required
  • Monthly or quarterly bills
  • Most UK homes still use these

2. Prepayment Meter

  • Pay-as-you-go (key or card)
  • Higher risk of overpaying if not compared
  • Can switch to credit meters

3. Economy 7 Meter

  • Day and night rates
  • Popular in flats and all-electric homes
  • Requires correct usage timing

All of these meter types can be compared without a smart meter. UtilityKing supports comparison for every meter type available in the UK market, ensuring you see all available tariffs regardless of your current setup.

Are Smart Meter Tariffs Cheaper?

The truth about "smart-only" pricing

Not Necessarily Cheaper

While some tariffs are labelled "smart-only," they are usually:

Time-of-Use Tariffs

Designed for EV charging or flexible usage patterns that may not suit typical households.

Limited Suitability

Often require lifestyle changes to maximize savings, which many households cannot accommodate.

Standard Fixed Tariffs

For most homes, standard fixed tariffs without smart meters are often cheaper and more predictable.

Key Insight: The majority of the cheapest fixed-rate tariffs in the UK market are available to all meter types, not just smart meters. Price competitiveness depends more on supplier competition and market conditions than on meter technology.

Fixed vs Variable Tariffs Without a Smart Meter

Your options remain fully available

Fixed Tariffs

  • Available without smart meters
  • Price certainty for 12-24 months
  • Often cheaper than the price cap
  • Protection against market increases

Variable Tariffs

  • Available to all meter types
  • Prices can rise with market changes
  • Less control over future costs
  • No exit fees for switching

Important: Smart meters are not a requirement for either fixed or variable tariffs. All suppliers must offer both options to customers with standard meters under Ofgem regulations. Your choice depends on your preference for price certainty versus flexibility.

Common Myths About Smart Meters and Comparison

Separating fact from fiction

  • "You can't switch without a smart meter"

    False — switching is completely independent of meter type. Millions switch annually with standard meters.

  • "Smart meters are compulsory"

    False — installation remains optional in the UK. You have the legal right to refuse a smart meter.

  • "Cheapest deals require a smart meter"

    False — many of the cheapest fixed deals in the market are available to standard meter customers.

  • "Smart meters save money automatically"

    False — savings come from switching to better tariffs, not from the meter itself.
