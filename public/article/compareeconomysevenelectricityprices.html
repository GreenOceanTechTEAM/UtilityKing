What Is Economy 7 Electricity?

Economy 7 is a two-rate electricity tariff that charges:

Cheaper night-time electricity for 7 hours

Lower rates during off-peak overnight hours.

More expensive daytime electricity for 17 hours

Higher rates during peak daytime hours.

It is designed for households that use a large proportion of electricity overnight, such as homes with storage heaters, immersion heaters, electric-only heating, or EV charging overnight.

Comparing Economy 7 electricity prices is essential because day and night rates vary significantly by supplier and postcode.

Economy 7 Day vs Night Rates Explained

Night Rates (Off-Peak)
30–60% Cheaper
  • Apply for 7 hours overnight
  • Exact times vary by region
  • Lower than standard tariffs
Day Rates (Peak)
Higher Than Standard
  • Apply for remaining 17 hours
  • Often higher than standard tariffs
  • Can wipe out night savings

To be cost-effective, most homes need to use at least 35–40% of electricity at night.

Economy 7 vs Standard Electricity: Which Is Cheaper?

Situation Economy 7 is Usually Cheaper Standard Tariff is Usually Cheaper
Usage Pattern Active night-time usage Mostly daytime usage
Heating Type Electric heating or EV charging Gas central heating
Lifestyle Night shift or timed usage Work from home
Property Type Flats with storage heaters Standard gas-heated homes

Who Should Still Use Economy 7 in 2026?

Economy 7 Works For:

  • Flats with storage heaters
  • Homes without gas supply
  • EV owners charging overnight
  • Properties with timed immersion heaters
  • Night-shift households

Consider Standard Tariffs:

  • Most usage during the day
  • Work from home arrangements
  • No longer have storage heaters
  • Low overnight electricity usage
