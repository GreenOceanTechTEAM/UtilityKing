Broadband for Streaming: What Speed Do You Need?

Streaming platforms require different speeds depending on quality.

Streaming QualityRecommended Speed (per device)
SD3–5 Mbps
HD10–15 Mbps
4K Ultra HD25 Mbps
If two people are watching Netflix in 4K at the same time, that's 50 Mbps already — before gaming, browsing, or video calls are considered.

For Streaming-Heavy Homes:
1–2 people → 50–100 Mbps
3–4 people → 150–300 Mbps
4+ users → 300 Mbps+

🎮

Broadband for Gaming: Why Latency Matters

For gamers, speed isn't everything. Latency (ping) is critical. Latency measures how quickly your device communicates with game servers. Lower is better.

Under 20msExcellent
20–40msVery good
40–60msPlayable
60ms+Noticeable lag

Full fibre connections typically offer lower latency than standard fibre.

When Faster Broadband Isn't the Problem

Sometimes issues aren't speed-related. Common causes of lag or buffering:

Poor Wi-Fi signal
Outdated router
Too many connected devices
Background downloads
Distance from router

Even gigabit broadband won't fix weak internal Wi-Fi coverage. Router quality matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q Is 100 Mbps enough for gaming?

Yes — for most gamers, 100 Mbps is sufficient if latency is low.

Q Does faster broadband reduce lag?

Not directly. Lower latency and stable infrastructure reduce lag more than raw speed.

Q Is full fibre better for gaming?

Yes. Full fibre typically offers lower latency and better stability.

Q What broadband speed is best for 4K streaming?

25 Mbps per stream minimum.

Q Is gigabit broadband worth it for gaming?

Usually unnecessary unless you have multiple heavy users or large downloads.
