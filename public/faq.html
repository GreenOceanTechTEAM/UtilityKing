Why switch gas and electricity providers?

You may compare gas and electricity costs separately, even though dual fuel is a common option to keep your energy in one package. You might perhaps uncover new bargains that give further savings by evaluating energy tariffs independently. Nobody likes to pay their bills too much in debt. Can I save money by switching energy suppliers?

You'll probably save money when you switch energy providers if you haven't done it in more than a year. The majority of gas and electricity providers give new customers significant discounts for the first 12 months. You will, however, be required to pay the usual fee after this discounted term expires, which is frequently substantially higher. A consumer who switches from a regular tariff to a more affordable one might save up to $500. Switching is simple.

What information do I need to switch energy supplier?

Just your zip code and a current electricity bill are required to change energy providers. If you don't have access to a physical copy of your bills, you might be able to discover the details you need by checking your emails or by visiting the website or mobile app of your energy provider.

What is the difference between fixed and variable-rate tariffs?

A "fixed price" electricity tariff guarantees that the cost of your gas and electricity units won't fluctuate throughout the course of the contract. Your energy cost may change during the course of the plan if it is variable. If your provider announces a price increase while you're on a fixed pricing plan, your rates won't change.

Can I change my electricity supplier if I have a smart meter?

The presence of a smart meter has no bearing on your ability to switch energy providers because all smart meters communicate in the same "language," regardless of who provides your electricity. The option to select between interval and non-interval services will remain available. However, the product plans and offers could fluctuate among various electrical providers. All of our dual fuel and electricity clients must adhere to this.

I'm moving home. How can I switch gas and electricity?

Finding a better price on your gas and electricity is a terrific opportunity when you move. The current energy provider will frequently start what is known as a "deemed contract" when you move into your new house. You might want to start looking around as soon as possible to find a better deal, as this can be one of the most expensive tariffs.

How often do energy prices change?

The price per kilowatt-hour is continually fluctuating as a result of the high expenses associated with producing and supplying power. There will still be fluctuation, but you may lock your tariff for a while by signing up for a fixed tariff energy plan.
